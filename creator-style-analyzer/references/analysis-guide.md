# Style Analysis Guide — Short-Form Video

## Your Job

Read every transcription. Then figure out what makes this creator's content work — specifically, what a copywriter would need to understand to produce scripts that achieve the same effects in their own voice.

There is no fixed framework. Every creator is different. Some creators are great because of their hooks. Some because of their story structure. Some because of their tone. Some because of something you won't expect until you read the transcriptions. Your job is to find what's actually there, not fill in a template.

## The One Rule

**Extract mechanics, not vocabulary.**

If a creator has a phrase they use often — that's surface. The question is: what is that phrase *doing*? What structural role does it play? Why does it work at that point in the script?

A copywriter reading your brief should understand the principles well enough to write in their OWN voice while achieving the same effects. If they could only succeed by copying the creator's specific words, you've written a bad brief.

## How to Approach It

1. **Read everything first.** Don't start writing the brief after 5 transcriptions. Read all of them. Let patterns emerge naturally.

2. **Notice what repeats across many videos.** A pattern that shows up in 2 videos is a coincidence. A pattern in 15+ videos is structural. Focus on the structural.

3. **Notice what's unusual.** Every creator does some things that are standard for the format. Don't waste space documenting the obvious. Focus on what this creator does differently from a generic short-form video.

4. **Ask "why does this work?"** For every pattern you identify, explain the mechanism. "They do X" is an observation. "They do X because it creates Y effect on the viewer" is an insight a copywriter can use.

5. **Acknowledge range.** Most creators have multiple modes. Don't flatten them into one formula. If they handle tech topics differently from opinion topics, say so.

## Output

Write a practical brief that a copywriter can use. Organize it by whatever dimensions actually matter for this specific creator — don't force a standard template. Each section should:

- Lead with the principle and why it works
- Show 2-3 concrete examples from the transcriptions
- End with actionable guidance

Close with a short checklist (8-12 items) a copywriter can run through to validate a draft.

The brief should feel like it was written by someone who deeply understands this specific creator — not like a generic "how to write short-form video" guide with the creator's name pasted in.
